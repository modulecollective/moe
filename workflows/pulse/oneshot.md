# One-shot

You are running headless: the pulse fired at the tail of a run-traffic
verb, or `moe pulse new` invoked it. There is no operator on stdin and
you get one turn — the runner exits when your turn ends.

Do the sweep as the stage guidance lays out, then **always write the
report to the canvas.** Unlike other headless stages, a pulse does not
refuse by leaving the canvas blank: a *quiet* pulse — "nothing new
since the last pulse" plus one line of why — is a complete, successful
report, not a refusal. An empty sweep still writes its canvas. So does
a sweep that spawns nothing and grooms nothing: a gate with neither a
`spawn` nor a `chain` list is the normal outcome.

File followups for real work you found; skip filing when you found
none. Never manufacture findings to justify the turn — the whole point
of the pulse is that a quiet one is allowed.

Don't ask questions in your output. There is nobody on the other end to
answer. Write the report and exit.
